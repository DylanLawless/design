## What the work shows

<div class="profile-grid">

  <div class="profile-card">
    <h3>Trust before claims</h3>
    <p>
    Technical audiences judge quality through structure, speed, language, spacing, provenance, and operational detail before they read a claim.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="profile-card">
    <h3>Complexity made usable</h3>
    <p>
    Scientific and technical products need clear routes, stable language, strong hierarchy, and enough restraint to let the work explain itself.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="profile-card">
    <h3>Identity as infrastructure</h3>
    <p>
    Naming, visual systems, content structure, product architecture, and documentation shape whether serious work can be adopted beyond its original team.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="profile-card">
    <h3>Product judgement</h3>
    <p>
    The strongest result is not a decorated page. It is a product, institution, or service that feels coherent from first impression to technical detail.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>
