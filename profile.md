<h2>Profile</h2>

<p>
I work at the intersection of statistical genomics, translational data science, scientific software, and regulated biomedical infrastructure. I have led analytical delivery across hospital, academic, and product-facing environments, including clinical genomics workflows, multi-omics studies, secure data systems, Bayesian methods, variant interpretation frameworks, and user-facing biomedical software.
</p>

<p>
My focus is evidence that can be defended: reproducible analyses, explicit uncertainty, traceable data systems, and software that supports decisions beyond the original research setting.
</p>

<section class="profile-section">

<h2>Selected experience</h2>

<h3>Universitäts-Kinderspital Zürich, senior scientist, 2023 to present</h3>

<p>
Genomics lead within the Swiss Pediatric Sepsis Study and SwissPedHealth National Data Stream. Built and delivered secure workflows linking whole-genome sequencing, multi-omics, electronic health record data, and high-performance computing across hospital settings.
</p>

<p>
Focus areas include rare disease diagnostics, paediatric sepsis, clinical genomics infrastructure, secure biomedical data delivery, and translational evidence systems.
</p>

<h3>EPFL Global Health Institute, senior postdoctoral scientist, 2018 to 2023</h3>

<p>
Developed statistical genomics and multi-omics workflows across sepsis, tuberculosis, chronic inflammatory disease, and translational cohort studies. Worked across EPFL, ETH Zurich, Swiss TPH, and international partner institutions.
</p>

<p>
Focus areas included genomic association studies, rare variant analyses, high-dimensional modelling, and integrative biomedical data analysis.
</p>

<h3>University of Leeds, PhD candidate, 2015 to 2018</h3>

<p>
Led genomic discovery and functional validation in rare immune disease in a translational hospital setting. Investigated severe clinical cases using genome sequencing, molecular biology, and clinical integration.
</p>

<p>
Work contributed to novel disease discovery and treatment-related findings in immune dysregulation, autoinflammation, and immunodeficiency.
</p>

<h3>ACM Global Laboratories, analytical scientist, 2014 to 2015</h3>

<p>
Worked in a regulated bioanalytical laboratory supporting global clinical trial programmes, biomarker workflows, and quality-controlled assay delivery.
</p>
